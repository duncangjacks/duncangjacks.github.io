1843 Oct 7 Birmingham Journal
BIRMINGHAM DISTRICT COURT OF BANKRUPTCY
I, EDGAR DUNN, at present and two years last past, residing at No. 19 in Freeman Street, in tha parish of Birmingham, and county of Warwick, and being a saddlers' ironmonger and retail brewer, and for five years immediately previous threrto residing at No. 85, in Park Street, in the parish aforesaid, and then carrying on the trade of a saddler's ironmonger only, DO HEREBY GIVE NOTICE, that I intend to present a petition to the Court of Bankruptcy, at the Birmingham District Court of Bankruptcy, in Birmingham aforesaid, praying to be examined touching my debts, estate, and effects; and to be protected from all process, upon making a full disclosure and surrender of such estate and affects, for payment of my just and lawful debts; and I hereby further give notice, that the time when the matter of the said petition shall be heard is to be advertised in the London Gazette, and in the Birmingham Journal newspaper, one month at the least after the date hereof. As witness my hand, this sixth day of October, in the year of our Lord one thousand eight hundred and forty-three. EDGAR DUNN.
Thomas Harding, Solicitor, Birmingham

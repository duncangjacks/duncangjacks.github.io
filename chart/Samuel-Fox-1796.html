Aris's Birmingham Gazette 3rd February 1834
SHERIFF'S COURT, BIRMINGHAM
On Friday (30th January) last, W.W. Lea, Esq. the Under Sheriff, held a Court at the Public Office for the recovery of debts under Â£20. The proceeding took place in the Court-room, and judging from the unusual number of attorneys and others present, the interest evinced in the permanent establishment of these new local courts, as affording the means of recovering debts with facility and at a considerable saving, was very appaent. There was only one Barrister, Mr. Scott, in attendance. The Court opened at ten o'clock, and the following persons were sworn on the Jury:- Mr. William Burt, Edgbaston, Foreman: Messrs. John Adkins, Deritend; Joseph Allen, Spiceal-street; Charles Bikker, Deritend; Luke Evans, Moor-street; Samuel Fox, Digbeth; Joseph Howes, Bordesley; William Lewis, High-street; Edward Latham, Bordesley; Joseph Price, Bull-street; John Porter, Edgbaston; and Edward Smallwood, Deritend. There were six causes entered for trial.
